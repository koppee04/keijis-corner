Do this daily, including rest days. Takes ~5 minutes. Consistency matters more than intensity.

Step 1
Ankle Alphabet
Trace A–Z with each foot in the air (seated). Builds mobility through full range.
Step 2
4-Way Isometrics
Push foot into wall or hand in all 4 directions (up, down, left, right). Hold 5–8s × 3 per direction, per foot.
Step 3
Single-Leg Balance
Wk 1–2: Flat floor, eyes open, 30s/leg
Wk 3–4: Folded towel, eyes open, 30s/leg
Wk 5–6: Folded towel, eyes closed, 20s/leg
Wk 7+: SL calf raise on towel, eyes open
Step 4
Short-Foot Exercise
Seated, press arch up without curling toes. Hold 5s × 10 reps per foot. Activates intrinsic foot muscles.
Ankle Care

Ice your right ankle for 10–15 min after any session where it feels aggravated. If sharp pain occurs during any exercise, stop that movement immediately.

Weeks 1–4 — Learning Phase

  • Focus on nailing form and tempo. Don't chase failure.
  • Use the low end of all rep ranges.
  • Nordic curls: 3–4 negatives per set is fine.
  • If 100 lb sandbag is too heavy, partially fill it or use the KB instead.

Weeks 5–8 — Building Phase

  • Push to the top of rep ranges before adding difficulty.
  • Add a rep or a set each week (not both).
  • Pike push-ups: elevate feet higher for more delt load.
  • Nordic curls: aim for 5–6 slow negatives, start catching yourself partway down.

Weeks 9–12 — Intensity Phase

  • Add a 4th set to your main compound lifts.
  • Start pausing at the bottom of squats and dips (2–3s holds).
  • Pull-ups: aim for unassisted full reps.
  • Consider filling the sandbag fully if you haven't already.

When to Add Difficulty (not more exercises)

  • You can complete the top of the rep range with clean form for 2 sessions in a row → make it harder.
  • Harder = slower tempo, longer pause, elevated feet, less assistance, more load.

😴 Sleep

6 hours is a limiting factor. Growth hormone releases primarily during deep sleep. Even getting to 7 hours will meaningfully improve recovery and muscle growth. Consider it part of your training.

🛑 Rest Days

Rest days are not optional. At beginner level with 6 hours of sleep, one full rest day per week is the minimum. If you feel unusually sore or fatigued, take two.

📉 Deload Every 4th Week

Drop to 2 sets per exercise instead of 3–4 and reduce intensity. This prevents overtraining and lets connective tissue catch up.

🦶 Ankle Care

Ice your right ankle for 10–15 min after any session where it feels aggravated. If sharp pain occurs during any exercise, stop that movement immediately.

Session Flow — Quick Reference

Every lower day: Warm-up (5 min) → 2 compound leg exercises → Nordic curls → 1 ankle/foot exercise → 1–2 core exercises → loaded carry or hold. ~50 min total.

Every upper day: Warm-up (5 min) → Pike push-up variation (delt priority) → 1 pull movement → 1 push movement → 1 delt isolation → 1 accessory. ~45 min total.

Always do the ankle rehab protocol (5 min) either before your warm-up or at the end of your day.
