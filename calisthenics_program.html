Horse Stance (Ma Bu)
Weeks 1–2
3 × 30s — High
Feet 2× shoulder width, toes slightly out. Thighs at ~45° to the floor. Back straight, chest up. Rest 30–45s between sets.
Weeks 3–4
3 × 45s — Mid
Begin sinking lower — aim for thighs between 45° and parallel. Knees track over second toe. Do not let knees cave inward.
Weeks 5–8
3 × 60–90s — Low
Thighs approaching parallel (classical depth). Quads and adductors will burn significantly — stay with it. Breathe: inhale 4 counts, exhale 4 counts.
Advanced
3 × 90s+ — Deep
Below parallel if mobility allows. Add arm work: guard position, slow punches, or hold a light kettlebell at chest. Extend holds to 2–3 min over months.
Ankle Protocol — Stage Gates
All exercises are bilateral and low-impact until cleared

Stage 1 (current): Bilateral only. No single-leg calf work or plyometrics. Tibialis raises and foot doming are rehabilitative — slow and precise.

Stage 2 (single-leg cleared): Progress to single-leg calf raises, remove bilateral-only restriction. Jump rope Phase 2 unlocks.

Stage 3 (plyometrics cleared): Add jump rope, pogo hops, bilateral jump squats. Jump rope Phase 3 unlocks. Reassess every 4–6 weeks.

Weeks 1–4 — Foundation

  • Focus on movement quality and pattern grooming before adding load.
  • Stop 2 reps short of failure on every set. Rest 90–120s.
  • If anything hurts (not burns), drop reps immediately.
  • Calf and foot work: slow and precise. Full range of motion always.
  • Nordic curls: 4 negatives at 5s lower. Push up with hands to return.

Weeks 5–8 — Development

  • Add weight or reps to key exercises (goblet squat, RDL, glute bridge).
  • Stop 1 rep short of failure. Rest 75–90s.
  • Push tempo on calf and core work — slower eccentrics.
  • Nordic curls: progress to 5–6 slower negatives, begin attempting partial concentric.
  • Horse stance: sink toward parallel depth. Quads and adductors will burn significantly.

Weeks 9–12 — Strength

  • Heavier loads, harder variations, shorter rest (60–75s).
  • True max effort on upper body sets. Full Nordic curl concentric attempts.
  • Upper body: once any set consistently exceeds 15 reps, add a weighted vest, sandbag, or kettlebell.
  • Calf raises: 4-4 tempo, step edge for full stretch. ROM before load.
  • Horse stance: 3 × 90s+ at parallel or below. Add kettlebell hold at chest.

Universal Progression Rules

  • Add load or +2 reps when all sets feel consistently easy with good form.
  • Timed holds: add 10 seconds when you hit the top of the prescribed range.
  • Nordic negatives: make the lower slower (aim for 8 sec) before attempting concentric work.
  • Calf raises: full range of motion always. A full-range bodyweight rep beats a partial-range loaded rep.
  • Deload weeks (Wk 4, 8, 12): keep all exercises, drop 1 set per block, reduce load ~10%.
Joint Pain vs. Muscle Fatigue

Sharp, localized pain always means stop. Diffuse burning fatigue is expected. If soreness persists beyond 48 hours, reduce volume on the affected day.

Rest Days

Training days are Mon / Tue / Thu / Fri. Do not stack similar muscle groups. Wed and the weekend are recovery — the morning routine can still run on rest days.

Deload Every 4th Week

Drop 1 set per block and reduce load by ~10%. Keep all exercises — the pattern work still matters. Weeks 4, 8, and 12 are natural deload checkpoints.

Disrupted Sleep or Appetite

These are signs of systemic fatigue. Take an extra rest day before the next session. Do not push through — connective tissue adapts more slowly than muscle and needs the recovery window.

Session Flow — Quick Reference

Day 1 & 2 (~45 min): Warm-up 5 min → main lower blocks → upper body finisher 10 min → mobility 5 min.

Day 3 (~45 min): Warm-up 5 min → calf/foot block (critical — slow and precise) → abductor + adductor blocks → mobility 5 min. No upper body.

Day 4 (~55 min): Warm-up 5 min → compound lower block → core block → upper body push emphasis → calf finisher 10 min → mobility 5 min. Heaviest loading day.

Morning routine (~15–27 min): Runs independently — activation + jump rope + horse stance + cool-down. See Ankle & Morning tab.
